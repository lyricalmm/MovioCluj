# Movio — Antigravity Website Brief

Use this brief to generate a clean presentation website for **Movio**, a student-focused bike-sharing startup from Cluj-Napoca.  
The website should feel credible, clear, and evidence-based. It should not sound like generic startup marketing.  
The main goal is to help the team look strong in **JA BizzFactory / GEN-E**, showing that Movio is not just an idea, but a project with validation, a defined pilot, and a realistic rollout plan.

---

## 1. One-line positioning

**Movio is a campus-first bike-sharing system for students in Cluj, built around fixed university-area hubs, a simple web-based unlocking flow, and a pricing model that stays accessible for users while being supported by sponsorships.**

---

## 2. What the website must communicate

The website should make 5 things clear:

1. **There is a real problem**  
   Short trips in Cluj are often either too expensive, too inconvenient, or poorly served by current options.

2. **Movio is simple to understand**  
   Students open the web app, find a nearby hub, unlock a bike, ride, and return it to a designated area.

3. **The idea has traction**  
   There is survey validation, real student interest, and a waiting list.

4. **The project is technically grounded**  
   The system uses classic city bikes, a smart-lock IoT module, geofenced hubs, and a lightweight web app.

5. **The business model is realistic**  
   Students pay a low annual subscription, while B2B sponsorship and branded hub partnerships support scale.

---

## 3. Tone and writing rules

- Write in **clear English**
- Keep the tone **direct, natural, and serious**
- Avoid hype words like: *revolutionary, disruptive, game-changing, world-class*
- Avoid exaggerated promises
- Prefer short paragraphs with real numbers
- The site should sound like a smart student team that understands both engineering and business
- Focus on evidence, logic, and clarity

## 5. The problem

The current mobility problem for students in Cluj is practical, not theoretical.

- Public bike systems are often seen as rigid, underused, or inconvenient
- Electric scooters are useful, but they become expensive for repeated short trips
- Students need something easier for daily movement between dorms, campuses, and nearby city points
- Existing options do not fully match the student routine: low-cost, fast, nearby, simple to unlock

Movio is built around that gap.

---

## 6. The solution

Movio is a bike-sharing system designed around student behavior.

### Core idea
- Fixed hubs placed near campuses, dormitories, and other high-traffic student areas
- Smart-lock system mounted on bicycles
- Access through a web app, without unnecessary friction
- Simple pricing that encourages frequent use
- A model that can start small and scale in controlled steps

### Technical concept
- Standardized city bikes
- Smart-lock IoT module
- Geofenced hub logic
- Web-based booking / access flow
- Expansion through additional hubs and bicycles instead of rebuilding the entire system

---

## 7. Validation and traction

Use these figures clearly on the website, ideally as large number cards.

### Survey data
- **121 total responses**
- **117 respondents are students in Cluj**
- **84 UTCN students**
- **26 UBB students**
- **6 USAMV students**
- **80.2% rated the idea 4 or 5 out of 5**
- **86.0% said they would use Movio at least occasionally if a hub was nearby**
- **49.6% said they would use it weekly or daily**
- **52 students left their email to join the pilot waitlist**
- **37.2% are willing to pay 20–50 RON per year**

### What students care about most
- Low price
- Bike availability
- First hour free
- A hub close to campus or dorm

### Main barriers identified
- Lack of bike lanes
- Cluj’s hills / topography
- Some students feel they do not need a bike
- A smaller share worry about theft or damaged bikes

## 9. Business model

The website should explain the model simply.

### B2C
- Annual student subscription
- Current pricing assumption: **29.99 RON / year**
- Includes **60 free minutes per day**
- Overage fee: **5 RON / hour**
- This layer drives usage, visibility, and student adoption

### B2B
- Sponsored bikes
- Branded hubs
- Employer branding for companies that want visibility near engineering and university campuses
- Future private mobility hubs for office buildings or partner locations

### Key message
Movio stays affordable for students because the model does not rely only on student payments.  
The B2B side helps fund growth while keeping the B2C side accessible.

## 11. Risks and how Movio answers them

Create a section called **Challenges and responses** with 3 to 4 simple cards.

### 1. Topography
Cluj is not flat, which affects bike movement and redistribution.  
**Answer:** controlled pilot areas, phased rollout, and operational redistribution.

### 2. Station imbalance
Bikes can accumulate in one direction during peak movement.  
**Answer:** relocation logic and user incentives to rebalance bikes.

### 3. Damage or theft
This is a common concern in shared mobility.  
**Answer:** smart-lock hardware, controlled pilot rollout, and clearly managed hub zones.

### 4. Adoption
Students will only use the service if it is simple and affordable.  
**Answer:** low price, web-based access, and hub placement near real student routes.
